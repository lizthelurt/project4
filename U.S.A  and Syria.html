In 2011, what became known as the arab spring revolts toppled Tunisian President Zine El Abidine Ben Ali and Egyptian President hosani murbak That March, peaceful protests sprouted in syria, after 15 boys were arrested and tortured for writting graffiti in supporting the Arab Spring. One of the boys, hamza al-khabeeet, was killed after having been tortured. The Syrian government, led by President Bashar al-Assad, responded to the protests by killing hundreds of demonstrators and imprisoning many more. In July 2011, defectors from the military announced the formation of the Free Syrian Army, a rebel group whose purpose to overthrow the government, and Syria began to slide into civil war.
